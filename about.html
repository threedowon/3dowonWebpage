3Dowon
Interactive Media Artist
Born 1996, South Korea

3Dowon is an interactive media artist who explores how people experience space by adding small changes to familiar objects. Starting from things easily encountered in everyday life — windows, books, sunlight, water, plants — he creates interactive environments that lead the audience to see familiar scenery anew through fresh senses.

He works with interactive installation, projection, Unreal Engine, and physical computing, combining sensors, embedded systems, real-time graphics, and software to connect digital media with physical space. Human movement and everyday behavior become interaction within the work, and simple gestures expand into a process of experiencing the entire space.

In 3Dowon's work, technology is not something to be shown off but a material for experience. Sensors, code, projection, and physical computing operate quietly behind the scenes, helping the audience immerse themselves in the space and the experience itself rather than the technology. His work leads people to slow their steps for a moment, trust the experience in front of them, and encounter familiar spaces through new senses.

Recently, he has continued to explore the relationship between familiar objects, memory, perception, and digital space. Creating a space where reality and imagination naturally meet, through moments when everyday experience briefly becomes unfamiliar, is at the center of his current practice.
